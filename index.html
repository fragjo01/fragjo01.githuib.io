Tennis Analytics — Case Study

How I Did It:

This project analyzed over 350,000 professional tennis matches from the ATP and ITF circuits to predict match duration. I used match-level statistics such as total points played, number of sets, aces, double faults, and court surface. To handle skewed durations, a log transformation was applied. Linear regression models, including interaction effects, were built and validated using residual diagnostics and cross-validation. The final model explains approximately 83% of match duration variation. Key insights include: more total points and service/return points lengthen matches, while aces and double faults shorten them. Clay courts tend to produce longer matches than grass or hard surfaces. The model provides strong predictive coverage, with 99.63% of observed durations falling within prediction intervals, offering actionable insights for players, coaches, and tournament organizers.
